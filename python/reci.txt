He added, that for a long time after the riots commenced, and until lately, the distrust of one
another was such, that even friends were affraid to communicate their sentiments to each other; That by
whispers this was brought about; and growing bolder as they became more communicative they found their
strength, and that there was a general disposition not only to acquiesce under, but to support the Laws--and
he gave some instances also of Magistrates enforcing them.
